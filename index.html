about.

I am a Glasgow-based developer who recently changed career into Tech having discovered a love for programming and am very excited about the new challenges ahead.

I previously worked within the Scottish Water Control Centre Development Team as a Developer / Business Analyst for 3 years after graduating from the University of Glasgow with a 2:1 BSc (Hons) in Geography and an MSc (with Merit) in Geospatial and Mapping Sciences.

I became interested in Software Development while working at Scottish Water and began learning programming in my spare time before undertaking the CodeClan immersive course in Software Development.

I am an enthusiastic developer who loves coding solutions to simple and complex problems. In my spare time I enjoy playing football with friends, snowboarding, running and traveling.

Please check out my GitHub projects and download a copy of my resume from the links within the navigation bar for further information.

interests.

As well as being a passionate Software Developer I love spending time hill walking Scotland's Munros and snowboarding in the mountains when I can. I am also a keen runner and grew up playing football from a very young age and still play 5-a-sides every Sunday with friends I went to primary school with!

I also relish the chance to travel and try new things. In 2013 I travelled to Hong Kong and Australia before spending a month and a half traveling around New Zealand on a figure of eight bus tour where I camped each night in remote locations and tried Bungy Jumping, Sky Diving, swimming with dolphins, whale watching, canyoning and white water rafting. I also managed to complete the Tongariro Alpine Crossing (19.4km) through the Tongariro National Park, a trek through an active volvanic hazard zone, and the Routeburn Track (32km) which passes through the Fiordland and Mount Aspiring National Parks.

In my spare time following a ski holiday I also like to put a video together using iMovie for my family and friends that were on the trip.
